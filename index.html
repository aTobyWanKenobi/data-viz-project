BREAKING NEWS
Overview
Welcome to Breaking News, an interactive visualization built around online news reports data. The goal of this website is to explore the evolution and distribution of important events around the globe over the course of a day, and understand which are the major online news sources that cover those events, and what are the geographical focus areas of those outlets.
Events
By clicking on the play button, you can start your journey through the events of November 5th, 2018 and see them appear on the world map. You can pause the simulation at any time and explore the events currently displayed on the map, or you can speed up and slow down the simulation according to your desired pace.
To visualize more information about a specific event, hover it with your cursor on the map. By clicking on the event, a new page opens with the first known full report covering it.
The underlying data originates from the widely known GDELT 2.0 dataset, which is a constantly and automatically updated collection of event data, where each event is categorized based on a precise coding scheme. You can select a subset of event categories to display in the map by checking the corresponding checkboxes. There are 4 major event categories, each one with multiple subcategories.
Sources
During the simulation, a new panel will take the place of this information section. Its purpose will be to display the 5 sources that have published the most reports about the events of that day. This top-5 is dinamycally updated as the hours go by. For each source, the cumulative number of written articles is showcased, along with a timeseries reporting the number of articles published during each 15 minutes update.
Clicking on a source object will switch the map view from the event-based perspective to a source oriented one, where the countries in which the source was active the most are highlighted.
